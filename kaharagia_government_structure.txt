
GOVERNMENT AND COURT STRUCTURE OF THE PRINCIPALITY OF KAHARAGIA
===============================================================

(This document consolidates and expands the entire structure, leadership roles,
functions, and operational details of the non-territorial government model for
the Principality of Kaharagia.)

---------------------------------------------------------------
I. THE SOVEREIGN (DŽOVERÁT)
---------------------------------------------------------------
The Sovereign is the supreme head of state, the guardian of national identity,
culture, and legitimacy, and the embodiment of the Kaharagian people. Even
without physical territory, the Sovereign maintains full sovereign dignity
and institutional authority.

Core Functions:
- Source of legal authority and constitutional legitimacy.
- Protector of the Kaharagian language, culture, symbols, and traditions.
- Fountain of Honour: grants all Orders, Decorations, and Medals.
- Supreme arbiter of heraldry and national symbolism.
- Accredits diplomats and authorises foreign relations.
- Commander of the Royal Kaharagian Army (future, symbolic until territory).

The Sovereign represents the unity and continuity of the Kaharagian nation and
delegates day-to-day governance to the Secretary of State.

---------------------------------------------------------------
II. THE ROYAL COURT OF KAHARAGIA
---------------------------------------------------------------
The Royal Court is the ceremonial, symbolic, and household institution that
supports the Sovereign personally. It is separate from the governmental
Secretariat of State.

A. Household of the Sovereign
- Master of the Household: oversees operations of the Court.
- Keeper of the Privy Purse: manages financial matters of the Court.
- Private Secretary to the Sovereign: handles personal correspondence.
- Court Archivist: maintains historical records of the Court.
- Director of Royal Residences (ceremonial/digital).

B. Royal Protocol (works closely with Secretariat Section I)
- Manages audiences, ceremonies, receptions, and precedence.
- Advises on dress codes, etiquette, ceremonial orders.
- Coordinates protocol for diplomatic visits.

C. Purveyors to the Royal Court
Companies or individuals granted a Royal Warrant to supply goods/services
of exceptional quality. The privilege is overseen jointly by:
- The Royal Chancellery (documentation and warrants)
- The Institute of Heraldry (design of warrant badges)

Purveryors are published annually in the Royal Gazette.

D. Court Officers and Honour Guards (future)
Ceremonial officers, Masters of Ceremonies, heralds, and symbolic guards.

---------------------------------------------------------------
III. THE SECRETARIAT OF STATE
---------------------------------------------------------------
The central governing body of Kaharagia, equivalent to a ministry-level body.
It coordinates all governmental functions, both internal and external.

A. Secretary of State
- Head of Government; primary advisor to the Sovereign.
- Oversees all Sections, Offices, and institutions.
- Ensures execution of laws, decrees, and national policy.

B. Assistant Secretary of State
- Manages day-to-day functioning.
- Ensures administrative continuity.
- Coordinates inter-office cooperation.

C. Deputy Secretaries of State (each leading a special institution)
1. Clerk of the Royal Chancellery
2. Secretary of the Chancellery of Honours
3. Chief Herald of Kaharagia

---------------------------------------------------------------
IV. SPECIAL INSTITUTIONS ATTACHED TO THE SECRETARIAT
---------------------------------------------------------------

1. Royal Chancellery
   Leadership: Clerk of the Royal Chancellery (Deputy Secretary of State)
   Roles:
   - Prepares, authenticates, and seals Royal Decrees, Letters Patent,
     Charters, and Warrants.
   - Keeper of the Great Seal.
   - Issues documents jointly with the Office of Government Communications
     for publication in the Royal Gazette.
   - Maintains the state archives of formal instruments.
   - Oversees the legislative assent process.

2. Chancellery of the Kaharagian Honours
   Leadership: Secretary of the Honours Chancellery
   Roles:
   - Administers Orders, Decorations, and Medals.
   - Maintains the Register of Honours.
   - Organises investitures and honours ceremonies.
   - Designs insignia with the Institute of Heraldry.
   - Advises the Sovereign on honours policy.

3. Institute of Heraldry
   Leadership: Chief Herald of Kaharagia
   Roles:
   - Grants and registers coats of arms, flags, badges.
   - Creates and maintains the Register of Arms, Flags, and Badges.
   - Designs state symbols, seals, banners, and heraldic devices.
   - Advises on identity, symbolism, and ceremonial representation.
   - Ensures heraldic law and cultural continuity.

---------------------------------------------------------------
V. SECTIONS OF THE SECRETARIAT OF STATE
---------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION I:
SECRETARIAT OF STATE — SECTION FOR GENERAL AFFAIRS & NATIONAL ADMINISTRATION
Leadership: Undersecretary of State for General Affairs

A. Office of Government Communications & Public Information
   - Publishes the Royal Gazette (with the Chancellery).
   - Coordinates official statements and announcements.
   - Oversees branding, design system compliance, public channels.
   - Ensures transparency and public access to state information.

B. Office of Citizenship, Identity & Naturalisation
   - Manages the National Register of Citizens.
   - Issues National Identification Numbers (NINs).
   - Handles naturalisation, renunciation, documentation.
   - Oversees identity documents and digital identity (in cooperation
     with Digital Government).

C. Office of Laws & Justice
   - Provides legal counsel to the Sovereign and Secretariat.
   - Drafts legislation, codification, constitutional review.
   - Oversees publication of laws and legal archives.
   - Ensures legal compliance of institutions.

D. Office of State Ceremonies & Protocol
   - Manages state ceremonies, national events, diplomatic protocol.
   - Coordinates precedence, ceremonial orders, and investitures.
   - Works with the Royal Court and the Honours Chancellery.

---------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION II:
SECRETARIAT OF STATE — SECTION FOR EXTERNAL RELATIONS & INTERNATIONAL PRESENCE
Leadership: Undersecretary of State for External Relations

A. Office of Foreign Affairs & Diplomatic Service
   - Maintains diplomatic missions (even if honorary).
   - Represents Kaharagia internationally.
   - Negotiates treaties (approved by Sovereign).
   - Provides consular-style services to nationals abroad.
   - Builds international partnerships.

B. Office of Defence Policy & National Security
   - Develops national security strategy.
   - Focuses on cyber-defence and sovereignty of digital systems.
   - Coordinates emergency planning and intelligence awareness.
   - Liaises with future military structures under the Sovereign.

---------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION III:
SECRETARIAT OF STATE — SECTION FOR CULTURAL, ACADEMIC & DIGITAL NATION-BUILDING
Leadership: Undersecretary of State for Culture & Digital Development

A. Office of Culture, Language & Heritage
   - Promotes the Kaharagian language and cultural identity.
   - Oversees museums, archives, heritage preservation.
   - Coordinates national cultural programs and symbolic projects.
   - Works closely with the Institute of Heraldry.

B. Office of Education, Science & Innovation
   - Develops national curricula, partnerships with educational bodies.
   - Oversees academic grants, research initiatives.
   - Supports digital learning platforms such as PastPortal.
   - Encourages scientific progress among nationals.

C. Office of Digital Government & Cybersecurity
   - Manages ePortal and digital infrastructure.
   - Oversees secure digital identity.
   - Protects government information systems.
   - Develops national digital sovereignty strategies.

---------------------------------------------------------------
VI. LEADERSHIP TITLES
---------------------------------------------------------------
- Secretary of State (Head of Government)
- Assistant Secretary of State
- Undersecretaries of State (Heads of Sections)
- Directors / Director-Generals (Heads of Offices)
- Deputy Secretaries of State (Heads of Special Institutions)
- Court Officers (Royal Household)
