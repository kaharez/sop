# XVIII. EXPANDED SECTION: ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF STATE

### 1. Purpose of the Office
The Assistant Secretary of State ensures the continuity of administrative
operations, supporting the Secretary of State and coordinating across the
entire Secretariat.

### 2. Duties (Expanded)
- Oversees workflow between Sections.
- Reviews documents before submission to the Secretary of State.
- Ensures that legal instruments meet procedural requirements.
- Manages crisis and continuity planning.
- Supervises internal reporting structures.
- Provides direction to Directors when Undersecretaries are unavailable.

### 3. Delegated Authority
The Sovereign and Secretary of State may delegate specific powers to the

### Assistant Secretary of State, including
- Endorsement of administrative notices.
- Interim approval of routine decisions.
- Coordination of emergency responses.

### 4. Oversight and workflow
- Maintains the Secretariat-wide tracker of submissions, deadlines, and
  publication status.
- Verifies that each instrument has legal review, Chancellery readiness, and
  communications approval before advancing to the Secretary of State.
- Chairs continuity planning sessions and tests COG procedures with Section II
  and Digital Government.

### 5. Metrics and assurance
- Monitors turnaround times for Gazette submissions, identity workflows, and
  ceremonial clearances; flags breaches of service standards.
- Coordinates remedial actions and documents lessons learned after incidents
  or delays.

### 6. Delegation limits and safeguards
- May issue interim approvals only within written delegations and must record
  all such actions for subsequent validation by the Secretary of State.
- Sensitive matters (security incidents, honours, heraldry) must be escalated
  immediately with a consolidated brief and proposed next steps.
