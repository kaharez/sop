# XLIII. PUBLIC ADMINISTRATION STANDARDS

## 1. Code of Conduct

- **Officials must uphold:**
- impartiality,
- professionalism,
- confidentiality,
- accountability.

## 2. Conflicts of Interest

- **An official must not:**
- participate in matters where personal interests conflict with duty,
- accept gifts or favours that could influence decisions,
- use government resources for personal benefit.

## 3. Ethical Obligations

- **Officials must:**
- report misconduct,
- avoid political influence in administrative duties,
- ensure transparency in decision making.

## 4. Performance Standards

- **All Offices must:**
- provide timely services,
- maintain accurate documentation,
- respond to public queries within reasonable timeframes,
- meet annual administrative targets.

## 5. Transparency and documentation
- Formal decisions and communications must be recorded with references to
  authority, date, and responsible official.
- Public-facing information should be clear, accessible, and aligned with
  Gazette publications to avoid inconsistency.

## 6. Training and compliance
- Mandatory induction on ethics, data protection, protocol, and publication
  standards for all officials and contractors.
- Periodic refreshers and attestation of compliance; breaches addressed via
  corrective action and, where necessary, disciplinary measures.
