# LXII. HERITAGE PROTECTION FRAMEWORK

1. Purpose
To preserve the cultural, symbolic, documentary, and ceremonial heritage of the
Principality for future generations.

2. Scope of Heritage Protection
A. Symbolic Heritage
National symbols, emblems, honours, regalia, and insignia.

B. Documentary Heritage
Decrees, registers, correspondence, archival materials, Gazette issues.

C. Cultural Heritage
Literature, traditions, recordings, artistic works, ceremonies.

3. Protection Measures
A. Archival Preservation
Digitisation, redundant storage, metadata tagging, and environmental controls.

B. Usage Restrictions
Official symbols must not be altered, misused, or commercialised without
permission.

C. Public Accessibility
Heritage materials should be made accessible unless restricted for legal or
privacy reasons.

4. Heritage Recognition Programmes
Institutions, scholars, or individuals may be recognised for contributions to:
- cultural preservation,
- research,
- education.

5. Risk management and security
- Maintain risk registers for heritage assets (physical and digital), covering
  environmental risks, cyber threats, and misuse of symbols.
- Coordinate with Defence Policy & Digital Government on protective measures
  for high-value or sensitive collections.

6. Access and engagement
- Provide curated public access (physical or digital) while protecting sensitive
  materials; publish finding aids and guides to improve discoverability.
- Promote heritage through exhibitions, talks, and publications, ensuring
  accuracy and respect for national identity.

7. Documentation standards
- All heritage items must have metadata (origin, date, provenance, rights) and
  authenticity verification where possible.
- Digitisation follows approved formats and includes checksums to ensure
  integrity over time.
