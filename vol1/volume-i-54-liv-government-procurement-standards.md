# LIV. GOVERNMENT PROCUREMENT STANDARDS

1. Purpose
To ensure fairness, ethical practice, and efficient use of resources when the
State procures goods or services.

2. Procurement Principles
A. Integrity
All procurement decisions must be made impartially.

B. Value
Selections must prioritise quality, reliability, and cost-effectiveness.

C. Transparency
Documented and reviewable procedures.

3. Procurement Categories
A. Routine Procurement
Supplies, administrative needs, digital services.

B. Specialist Procurement
Technical contracts, cultural materials, heraldic production.

C. Purveyor Appointments
Providers formally recognised by Royal Warrant.

4. Ethical Requirements
Suppliers must adhere to standards on:
- labour practices,
- sustainability,
- cultural respect,
- confidentiality.

5. Tendering and Documentation
Proposals must include:
- description of service,
- technical specifications,
- pricing,
- compliance declarations.

6. Process controls
- Competitive process preferred; single-source justified in writing and
  approved by the relevant Undersecretary (or Secretary for high-value items).
- Evaluation criteria documented and applied consistently; conflicts of
  interest declared and managed.
- Contracts include performance standards, data/security requirements, and
  termination clauses for breach.

7. Transparency and records
- Procurement records (requirements, bids, evaluations, awards) are retained
  per retention schedules; summaries may be published where appropriate.
- Purveyor appointments follow warrant procedures and are reviewed annually.
