# XIV. EXPANDED SECTION: CHANCELLERY OF HONOURS

## 1. Structure

- **The Chancellery consists of:**
- Secretary of the Honours Chancellery
- Registrar of Honours
- Ceremonial Director
- Insignia and Manufacturing Coordinator
- Research Officers

## 2. Honours System Governance (Expanded)

- **The Chancellery ensures:**
- Fair and transparent nomination procedures.
- Maintenance of detailed individual records.
- Preservation of historical information about every award.
- Manages honours categories, grades, and post-nominals; issues updates to
  ensure consistency and avoid overlap.
- Oversees conflict-of-interest checks and eligibility verification before
  submission to the Honours Council and Sovereign.
- Publishes honours lists and citations in the Gazette; maintains public
  summaries and restricted service notes where necessary.

## 3. Insignia Standards (Expanded)

- **All insignia must meet standards of:**
- Durability
- Artistic quality
- National symbolism
- Consistency with heraldic rules

The Institute of Heraldry provides oversight of heraldic elements in insignia.
- Production runs are catalogued with batch numbers; master reference sets are
  held in the archives with secure storage and environmental controls.
- Replacement protocols require verification of loss/damage and reissue
  records in the Register of Honours.

## 4. Ceremony Protocol (Expanded)

- **Investitures must follow:**
- Approved ceremonial forms
- Order of precedence
- Proper dress codes
- Correct usage of flags, symbols, and instruments
- Accessibility and inclusion: ceremonies accommodate mobility, language, and
  cultural needs; briefings provided to recipients in advance.
- Records: every investiture generates a ceremonial record (programme, seating,
  citations) deposited with the National Archives.
