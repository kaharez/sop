# V. SPECIAL INSTITUTIONS ATTACHED TO THE SECRETARIAT

## 1. Royal Chancellery (Deputy Secretary of State)

- **Responsibilities:**
- Drafting, registration, sealing, and issuance of Royal Decrees.
- Custodianship of the Great Seal.
- Certification of official instruments.
- Publication coordination with the Office of Government Communications.
- Maintains the master Register of Instruments and ensures metadata,
  signatures, and seals meet authenticity requirements.
- Oversees version control, repeal tracking, and archiving of superseded texts.
- Provides procedural guidance to all Offices on format, numbering, and
  submission protocols.

## 2. Chancellery of Honours (Deputy Secretary of State)

- **Responsibilities:**
- Managing Orders, Decorations, and Medals.
- Maintaining the Register of Honours.
- Organising investiture ceremonies.
- Advising the Sovereign on honours matters.
- Verifies nominations for eligibility, conflict-of-interest risks, and
  completeness before Council review.
- Coordinates production, custody, and replacement of insignia; ensures all
  insignia conform to heraldic standards.
- Publishes honours lists in the Gazette and ensures citations are preserved
  for public record and archival integrity.

## 3. Institute of Heraldry (Deputy Secretary of State)

- **Responsibilities:**
- Granting arms, flags, and badges.
- Maintaining national heraldic registers.
- Overseeing heraldic design standards.
- Advising on national symbolism.
- Issues binding guidance on the use, protection, and reproduction of state
  symbols across physical and digital media.
- Reviews and approves heraldic devices for purveyors and government Offices,
  ensuring consistency with national identity.
- Maintains authoritative digital assets (vector files, colour specifications)
  for all official symbols and seals.
