# XXVI. EXPANDED SECTION: OFFICE OF DEFENCE POLICY & NATIONAL SECURITY

### 1. Purpose
This Office develops the Principalityâ€™s security policy, focusing on strategic
planning, cyber defence, and emergency preparedness.

### 2. Responsibilities

#### A. Strategic Policy

### Drafts national security strategies aligned with
- digital sovereignty,
- international cooperation,
- future territorial considerations.

#### B. Cyber Security

### Protects
- official communication systems,
- digital infrastructure,
- databases containing citizen and institutional information.

#### C. Emergency Preparedness

### Plans for
- continuity of governance,
- safeguarding essential records,
- emergency communication protocols.

#### D. Defence Liaison
Coordinates policy with future military or defence structures while respecting
their independent command under the Sovereign.

### 3. Incident response and coordination
- Maintains national incident response playbooks covering cyber, communications,
  and administrative crises.
- Coordinates joint exercises with Digital Government and Government
  Communications to validate readiness and public messaging.
- Establishes escalation thresholds and notification timelines to the Secretary
  of State and Sovereign.

### 4. Intelligence and threat monitoring
- Aggregates threat intelligence relevant to digital sovereignty and national
  identity systems; disseminates actionable insights to relevant Offices.
- Ensures classified handling complies with data protection and access control
  standards.

### 5. Records and accountability
- Documents all incidents, lessons learned, and remediation actions; reports
  periodically to the Secretary of State.
- Ensures alignment with Continuity of Government measures (Section XXXVII).
