# XLVIII. INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS FRAMEWORK

### 1. Principles of International Conduct
Although the Principality is non-territorial, it may engage in diplomacy,

### cultural exchange, and international cooperation in accordance with
- peaceful engagement
- respect for sovereignty
- the promotion of shared values
- constructive cooperation

### 2. Treaty Procedure

#### A. Negotiation
Conducted by the Office of Foreign Affairs.

#### B. Approval

### Requires
- review by the Secretary of State
- formal assent by the Sovereign

#### C. Registration

### Treaties must be
- registered by the Royal Chancellery
- published in the Gazette
- archived permanently

### 3. Representation Abroad

### Kaharagia may maintain
- special envoys
- honorary consuls
- cultural representatives

### All representatives must be
- accredited by the Sovereign
- bound by diplomatic protocol
- trained in ceremonial and legal standards

### 4. Cultural Diplomacy

### Used to strengthen national identity abroad
- exhibitions
- educational partnerships
- cultural programmes
- participation in global conferences

### 5. International Cooperation

### Areas of cooperation include
- humanitarian initiatives
- digital governance innovation
- arts and heritage preservation
- educational exchanges

### 6. Representation protocols
- All representatives (envoys, consuls, cultural delegates) must receive
  briefing on protocol, identity safeguards, and communication standards.
- Activities conducted abroad are reported to Foreign Affairs and, where
  relevant, published in the Gazette for transparency.

### 7. Documentation and archiving
- Agreements, MOUs, and cultural programme outlines are registered with the
  Royal Chancellery and preserved in the International Affairs Repository.
- Diplomatic engagements of public significance generate summaries for the
  Gazette to maintain visibility and legitimacy.
