# XLIX. CRISIS MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK

### 1. Purpose

### To ensure organised, swift, and lawful action during emergencies that threaten
- governmental continuity
- national identity systems
- digital infrastructure
- communications networks

### 2. Crisis Classifications

#### A. Administrative Crisis
Failure of internal systems or personnel incapacity.

#### B. Digital Crisis
Cyberattack, system corruption, or critical data loss.

#### C. Communications Crisis
Loss of external or internal communication channels.

#### D. Reputation or Public Confidence Crisis
Arising from misinformation, defamation, or public misunderstanding.

### 3. Emergency Authorities

### The Sovereign may authorise temporary measures including
- emergency issuance of identity documents
- expedited communications protocols
- activation of Continuity of Government structures

### All emergency powers
- must be proportionate
- must be time-limited
- must be publicly recorded

### 4. Crisis Response Procedures

#### A. Initial Assessment
Conducted by the relevant Office.

#### B. Notification
Secretary of State must be informed immediately.

#### C. Coordination Meeting

### Within 12 hours, involving
- Secretary of State
- Deputy Secretaries
- Lead Office

#### D. Public Statement
Issued by the Office of Government Communications to maintain transparency.

### 5. Restoration of Normal Operations

### After a crisis
- emergency measures are revoked
- full reports are prepared
- systems are validated before resuming normal function

### 6. Documentation and audit
- All actions, approvals, and communications during a crisis are logged with
  timestamps; critical decisions are later confirmed in writing.
- A post-incident report documents root causes, impacts, and corrective
  measures; summaries are published when appropriate.

### 7. Coordination with continuity
- If COG is activated, crisis response aligns with continuity protocols for
  authority, authentication, and publication.
- Temporary measures affecting identity, honours, or ceremonial practice are
  regularised or revoked promptly once normal operations return.
