# XVII. EXPANDED SECTION: SECRETARY OF STATE

### 1. Office and Authority
The Secretary of State is the Sovereign’s principal advisor and the primary
coordinator of national governance. The Secretary of State ensures that the
Sovereign’s decisions are implemented consistently across all Sections and
Special Institutions.

### 2. Responsibilities (Expanded)

#### A. Strategic Leadership
- Defines national priorities and long-term objectives.
- Oversees execution of Royal Decrees by relevant Offices.

#### B. Institutional Stewardship
- Ensures effective functioning of Sections and Directors.
- Maintains professional standards and institutional continuity.

#### C. Intergovernmental Coordination
- Facilitates communication among Undersecretaries and Directors.
- Integrates administrative and ceremonial responsibilities.

#### D. Diplomatic and External Representation

### Represents the Principality in
- International forums
- Diplomatic engagements
- Intergovernmental communications

#### E. Custodianship of National Documentation
Works closely with the Royal Chancellery to ensure the legitimacy and
procedural integrity of legal instruments.

### 3. Decision-making framework
- Authority basis: every decision references the Sovereign’s delegated powers
  or a specific Royal Decree.
- Consultation: legal review, protocol check, and communications plan are
  required before sign-off on major acts.
- Publication: no decision is effective until recorded in the Register and, if
  applicable, published in the Gazette.

### 4. Delegation and escalation
- Routine matters may be delegated to the Assistant Secretary of State with
  written parameters; sensitive items (security, honours, identity) require
  personal clearance.
- Escalation paths are documented for disputes between Sections; unresolved
  issues are decided by the Secretary with reasons recorded.

### 5. Engagement and representation
- Serves as principal spokesperson for governmental matters (distinct from the
  Sovereign’s ceremonial voice).
- Represents the Principality in bilateral and multilateral settings, ensuring
  all statements align with national identity and legal commitments.

### 6. Accountability and reporting
- Receives monthly Section performance reports and risk updates; issues
  directives with timelines.
- Provides annual state of administration report for the Sovereign and the
  Gazette to maintain transparency with citizens.
