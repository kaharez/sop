# XXXIX. NATIONAL SYMBOL PROTOCOL

### 1. Purpose
National symbols represent the dignity and unity of the Principality and must
be used with correctness and respect.

### 2. National Flag Regulations
- Must be displayed prominently during national events.
- Must not be defaced or altered.
- Must not be used in commercial advertising without permission.
- Must be retired respectfully when worn.

### 3. Royal Arms

### May be used only by
- the Sovereign,
- the Royal Court,
- the Royal Chancellery,
- the Secretariat of State,
- authorised institutions.

### 4. Government Emblems
Offices use approved insignia provided by the Institute of Heraldry.

### 5. Digital Use of Symbols

### Digital versions must
- meet design standards,
- use official colour codes,
- be displayed without distortion.

### 6. Protection and enforcement
- Unauthorised use or alteration of protected symbols triggers corrective
  action; the Institute of Heraldry and Office of Laws & Justice coordinate
  responses and notices.
- Commercial use requires prior approval and, where applicable, a licence or
  purveyor warrant.

### 7. Custody and quality control
- Master artwork and specifications are maintained by the Institute of
  Heraldry; derivative assets must be verified against masters.
- Worn or damaged physical symbols (flags, standards) must be retired and
  replaced; retirement is conducted respectfully.
