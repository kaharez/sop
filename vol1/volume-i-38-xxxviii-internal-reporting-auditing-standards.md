# XXXVIII. INTERNAL REPORTING & AUDITING STANDARDS

## 1. Annual Reporting

- **Each Office must submit an annual report including:**
- major activities,
- legal instruments processed,
- cultural or diplomatic initiatives,
- cybersecurity incidents,
- financial summaries (if applicable).

## 2. Review by the Secretary of State

- **Reports are examined by:**
- Secretary of State
- Assistant Secretary of State
- Relevant Undersecretaries

## 3. Auditing

- **Periodic audits ensure:**
- accuracy of records,
- compliance with procedures,
- protection of sensitive information,
- ethical conduct.

## 4. Corrective Actions

## If irregularities are found, the responsible Office must

- correct documentation,
- revise procedures,
- undergo follow-up review.

## 5. Cadence and scope
- Monthly: key metrics (turnaround times, publication completeness, incident
  counts) reported by each Office to its Undersecretary.
- Quarterly: compliance reviews on legal publication, data protection, and
  protocol adherence.
- Annual: full audit cycle including finance (where applicable), identity and
  honours registers, Gazette integrity, and archival health.

## 6. Evidence and traceability
- All reports include references to source registers, hashes for digital
  records, and confirmation of archival deposits.
- Audit working papers are retained per retention schedules and access-controlled.

## 7. Issue management
- Findings are assigned owners and deadlines; remediation is tracked and
  verified in follow-up reviews.
- Significant issues (security, identity, honours, legal publication) are
  escalated to the Secretary of State and noted in the Gazette if public impact
  is material.
