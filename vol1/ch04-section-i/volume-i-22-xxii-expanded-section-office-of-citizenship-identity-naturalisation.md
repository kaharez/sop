# XXII. EXPANDED SECTION: OFFICE OF CITIZENSHIP, IDENTITY & NATURALISATION

## 1. Purpose
Responsible for the administration of citizenship, national identity, and
issuance of National Identity Numbers (NINs).

## 2. Responsibilities
- Managing citizenship applications.
- Maintaining the National Register of Citizens.
- Issuing and verifying identity documentation.
- Overseeing naturalisation ceremonies.
- Coordinating digital identity systems.

## 3. NIN Governance (Expanded)

- **The Office must:**
- Assign NINs according to established rules.
- Maintain accurate serial counters.
- Provide certified records upon request.
- Protect data integrity through secure protocols.

## 4. Citizenship Integrity

- **The Office ensures:**
- Thorough vetting of applicants.
- Prevention of duplicate identities.
- Legal compliance with citizenship regulations.

## 5. Processes and controls
- Application intake: via ePortal or authenticated channels; identity proofing
  and eligibility checks recorded with audit trails.
- Decision making: approvals/denials documented with reasons; appeal pathways
  published and tracked.
- Data quality: periodic deduplication, error correction, and reconciliations
  with Digital Government to maintain a clean Register.

## 6. Anti-fraud and security
- Implements multi-factor verification for digital identity issuance.
- Conducts fraud monitoring and incident response with Digital Government and
  Defence Policy offices; suspicious patterns escalated promptly.
- Access to the Register follows role-based permissions and logging.

## 7. Naturalisation and ceremonies
- Schedules and conducts ceremonies with the Office of State Ceremonies &
  Protocol; issues certificates and updates the Register on completion.
- Publishes non-sensitive statistics and ceremonial notices in the Gazette for
  transparency.
