# XXIV. EXPANDED SECTION: OFFICE OF STATE CEREMONIES & PROTOCOL

## 1. Purpose
This Office manages the ceremonial life of the Principality, ensuring that
events involving the Sovereign or state institutions are conducted with dignity,
precision, and adherence to national tradition.

## 2. Ceremonial Responsibilities
- Organising investitures and honours ceremonies.
- Planning national commemorations.
- Overseeing ceremonies of state significance.
- Preparing event scripts, programmes, and ceremonial orders.
- Maintaining ceremonial regalia, flags, and symbolic objects.

- **Execution standards:**
- Each event has a documented run sheet, rehearsal plan, and contingency plan.
- All regalia and flags must be condition-checked and authenticated before use.
- Ceremonial photography/recording must follow protocol on placement, dignity,
  and archival delivery.

## 3. Protocol Responsibilities

### A. Precedence

- **Maintains the official table of precedence for:**
- Royal Court dignitaries,
- Secretariat of State officials,
- distinguished nationals and foreign guests.

### B. Hosting Duties

- **Prepares:**
- receptions,
- formal audiences,
- diplomatic events,
- small-scale ceremonial interactions.

### C. Dress Codes

- **Ensures appropriate attire for officials at:**
- state ceremonies,
- formal occasions,
- honours events.

### D. Ceremonial Documents
Issues formal invitations, seating charts, and ceremonial instructions.

## 4. Relationship with Other Bodies
- Works closely with the Chancellery of Honours for investitures.
- Coordinates with the Institute of Heraldry for regalia design.
- Consults the Royal Court for major ceremonial occasions.

## 5. Accessibility, inclusion, and security
- Ensures venues and programmes accommodate mobility, language, and cultural
  needs; provides guidance to invitees in advance.
- Coordinates with Defence Policy & National Security and Digital Government
  for physical and cyber security of events.

## 6. Records and publication
- Files ceremonial scripts, seating charts, and programmes with the National
  Archives and Royal Chancellery.
- Publishes ceremonial notices, orders of dress, and precedence updates in the
  Gazette where applicable.
