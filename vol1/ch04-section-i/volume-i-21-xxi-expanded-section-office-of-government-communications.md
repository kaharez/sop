# XXI. EXPANDED SECTION: OFFICE OF GOVERNMENT COMMUNICATIONS

## 1. Purpose
The Office ensures that the Principalityâ€™s official communications are clear,
consistent, and accessible.

## 2. Responsibilities (Expanded)
- Drafting public statements.
- Managing official websites and digital publications.
- Publishing the Royal Gazette.
- Coordinating public information campaigns.
- Ensuring compliance with branding and design guidelines.

## 3. Gazette Authority

- **The Office has exclusive authority to:**
- Schedule Gazette publications.
- Validate submissions from other Offices.
- Maintain the digital archive.
- Ensure that legal instruments meet publication standards.

## 4. Coordination with Royal Chancellery

- **No document may be published until:**
- Authenticated by the Clerk of the Royal Chancellery.
- Registered correctly.
- Assigned a Gazette reference number.

## 5. Channels and standards
- Maintains official web, ePortal notices, and verified social channels; all
  content mirrors the authenticated Gazette text.
- Enforces accessibility (readability, contrast, language) and brand fidelity
  across all outputs.
- Uses plain language guidelines to maximise clarity of public information.

## 6. Crisis and reputation management
- Leads public-facing response during incidents in coordination with Section II
  (security) and the Assistant Secretary of State.
- Issues holding statements, updates, and closures with timestamps and source
  authority to maintain trust.
- Monitors for misinformation affecting state identity; coordinates corrective
  notices with the Office of Laws & Justice where necessary.

## 7. Archival and integrity
- Maintains permanent URLs, cryptographic hashes, and backups for all Gazette
  issues and statements.
- Provides certified copies and ensures metadata (issuing authority, date,
  reference number) is intact for legal reliance.
