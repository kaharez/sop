# XXXV. EXPANDED SECTION: ROYAL COURT — OPERATIONAL DETAIL

## 1. Daily Operations

## The Royal Court follows an established schedule, typically including
- review of correspondence,
- preparation of ceremonial documents,
- planning of events,
- coordination with Secretariat of State.

## 2. Court Communications

## All communications must
- reflect the dignity of the Court,
- use approved formats,
- be properly archived.

## 3. Management of Visitors

## Visitors must
- register with Court officials,
- follow dress and behaviour standards,
- observe any restricted areas.

## 4. Internal Conduct

## Court officers must
- maintain confidentiality,
- demonstrate impartiality,
- avoid commercial or political entanglements.

## 5. Scheduling and readiness
- Maintains daily and weekly schedules aligned with ceremonial commitments and
  Sovereign engagements; changes logged with time and reason.
- Venue readiness checks cover protocol layout, security, accessibility, and
  regalia availability.

## 6. Records and archiving
- Files correspondence, visitor logs, event programmes, and ceremonial scripts
  with the Royal Chancellery/National Archives according to classification.
- Uses approved digital systems for tasking and calendar management; access is
  role-based and audited.

## 7. Security and access
- Coordinates with Defence Policy and Digital Government on physical and cyber
  controls for Court premises and systems.
- Enforces visitor screening, badge issuance, and escorted movement in
  restricted areas.

## 8. Training and standards
- Court officers receive onboarding for etiquette, confidentiality, data
  protection, and anti-corruption standards.
- Regular refreshers on protocol updates, symbol usage, and publication rules
  ensure consistency across events.
