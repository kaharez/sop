# XXV. EXPANDED SECTION: OFFICE OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS & DIPLOMATIC SERVICE

## 1. Purpose
The Office manages external relations and represents Kaharagia internationally.
Even without territorial jurisdiction, the Principality maintains sovereign
capacity to engage in diplomacy and cooperative relations.

## 2. Responsibilities

### A. External Representation
- Oversees diplomatic appointments.
- Coordinates official communications with foreign governments.
- Prepares letters of credence, recall, and diplomatic notes.

### B. Treaty Management

## Responsible for
- negotiation of agreements,
- maintenance of treaty archives,
- advising on international obligations.

### C. Service to Nationals Abroad

## Assists citizens by
- providing certificates or confirmations,
- facilitating honorary consular networks,
- guiding nationals in crisis.

### D. International Presence

## Promotes awareness of Kaharagia’s
- cultural identity,
- digital sovereignty model,
- national achievements and initiatives.

## 3. Diplomatic Communications Standards

## All communications must follow
- established diplomatic formats,
- formal tone,
- clear lines of authority,
- Royal Chancellery authentication.

## 4. Credentialing and records
- Maintains a registry of accredited representatives and mission statuses.
- Issues, records, and archives letters of credence/recall with the Royal
  Chancellery; publishes accreditation notices in the Gazette.

## 5. Protocol and coordination
- Works with State Ceremonies & Protocol for visits, receptions, and audience
  arrangements; aligns seating and precedence with national tables.
- Coordinates with Defence Policy & National Security on security assessments
  for engagements and with Government Communications on public messaging.

## 6. Consular-style services (honorary model)
- Provides certification and assistance to nationals abroad through honorary
  consuls or designated representatives.
- Maintains crisis contact procedures and verification protocols for citizens’
  status.
