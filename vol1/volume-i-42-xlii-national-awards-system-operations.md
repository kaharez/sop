# XLII. NATIONAL AWARDS SYSTEM OPERATIONS

1. Purpose
The awards system recognises outstanding service, merit, or achievement in
public, cultural, humanitarian, or sovereign service.

2. Award Categories
A. Orders of Merit
For extraordinary contributions to the nation.

B. Decorations
For distinguished service in specific areas (e.g., humanitarian service).

C. Medals
For sustained contributions, long service, or specific acts of merit.

3. The Honours Council
A. Composition
Includes senior officials, experts, and distinguished citizens.

B. Duties
- Reviewing nominations
- Advising the Sovereign
- Recommending honours categories and recipients

4. Nomination Procedures
- Nominations may be public or internal.
- The Honours Chancellery verifies eligibility and achievements.
- The Council deliberates and submits recommendations to the Sovereign.

5. Investiture Ceremonies
- Conducted by the Sovereign or a delegated official.
- Recipients are formally presented with insignia.
- The event is recorded and published in the Gazette.

6. Record Keeping
All honours must be entered into the Register of Honours:
- date of conferment,
- category and grade of honour,
- citation text,
- insignia issued.

7. Compliance and integrity
- Conflict-of-interest and eligibility checks are mandatory before Council
  review; adverse findings are documented with outcomes.
- Lost or replaced insignia are recorded with serial/batch references.
- Appeals or requests for correction (name, citation) follow a documented
  process and are logged in the Register.
