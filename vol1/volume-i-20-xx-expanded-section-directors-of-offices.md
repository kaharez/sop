# XX. EXPANDED SECTION: DIRECTORS OF OFFICES

### 1. Function
Directors lead functional Offices within each Section and are responsible
for highly specialised areas of governance. Directors are expected to
demonstrate professional expertise, reliability, and discretion.

### 2. Core Duties

#### A. Operational Leadership
- Direct daily activities of the Office.
- Develop annual plans and performance metrics.
- Ensure accurate documentation and reporting.

#### B. Technical Authority

### Directors must be experts in their fields, capable of
- Drafting official communications.
- Ensuring procedural compliance.
- Advising Undersecretaries of State.
- Representing the Office in formal settings.

#### C. Document Management

### Directors prepare or certify documents destined for
- Royal Chancellery
- Chancellery of Honours
- Secretariat leadership
- Royal Gazette

### 3. Professional Standards

### Directors must
- Maintain confidentiality.
- Demonstrate impartiality.
- Uphold the dignity of the Principality.
- Avoid conflicts of interest.
- Follow digital security protocols strictly.

### 4. Delivery and oversight
- Set and monitor key performance indicators for their Office; report monthly
  to the Undersecretary with risks and mitigation plans.
- Ensure staff training on protocol, data protection, and publication standards.
- Maintain auditable records of decisions, approvals, and communications.

### 5. Ethics and conflicts
- Declare potential conflicts of interest (including vendor or purveyor ties)
  and recuse where necessary.
- Enforce anti-corruption and procurement standards within the Office.

### 6. Continuity and succession
- Identify deputies and ensure role handover packs exist for critical posts.
- Maintain backup procedures for core services and datasets in line with
  continuity and cybersecurity requirements.
