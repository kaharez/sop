# XXXIV. EXPANDED SECTION: NATIONAL ARCHIVES & DOCUMENT PRESERVATION

## 1. Custodianship

- **The Royal Chancellery is the custodian of:**
- Royal Decrees,
- Letters Patent,
- Charters,
- Warrants,
- Official registers.

- **The Office of Government Communications preserves:**
- Gazette issues,
- public documents,
- digital publications.

## 2. Archival Requirements

- **Documents must be:**
- stored securely,
- indexed,
- certified,
- preserved in both physical (if applicable) and digital forms.

## 3. Access to Records

- **Archives must provide:**
- public access to non-sensitive items,
- restricted access for sensitive materials,
- certified copies upon request.

## 4. Long-Term Preservation

- **Digital preservation includes:**
- redundancy across multiple systems,
- cryptographic authenticity checks,
- periodic format migration to maintain accessibility.

## 5. Metadata and integrity
- Each record must include provenance, issuing authority, date, reference
  number, classification, and hash where applicable.
- Integrity checksums are verified on a schedule; anomalies trigger incident
  response and restoration from trusted backups.

## 6. Disposal and retention
- Follows approved retention schedules; destruction of records requires
  two-person approval, logging, and (for sensitive items) certificate of
  destruction.
- Permanent records (Gazette, honours, citizenship, heraldry) are never
  destroyed and are mirrored across archival tiers.

## 7. Access management
- Role-based permissions apply to restricted archives; access is logged.
- Public access is delivered via authenticated portals where needed, ensuring
  the public view matches certified versions.
