# XXXII. EXPANDED SECTION: PROTOCOL PROCEDURES

## 1. Audiences with the Sovereign

- **Requests must be submitted through:**
- Private Secretary (for personal matters)
- Office of State Ceremonies & Protocol (for formal matters)
- Secretary of State (for governmental matters)

## 2. Invitation Procedures

- **Official invitations must include:**
- the name of the Sovereign,
- full event details,
- dress code,
- seating designation,
- signature or seal of the authorising office.

## 3. Forms of Address

### A. The Sovereign

- **Addressed as:** “Your Serene Highness” or “Your Highness”

### B. Secretary of State

- **Addressed as:** “Secretary of State” or “Madam/Mister Secretary”

### C. Undersecretaries
“Undersecretary”

### D. Directors
“Director”

### E. Court Officers
“Master of the Household”, “Private Secretary”, etc.

## 4. Correspondence Protocol

- **All official correspondence must:**
- use approved templates,
- include the appropriate header and insignia,
- be registered with the Royal Chancellery if formal,
- follow diplomatic style for foreign communications.

## 5. Invitations and guest management
- Invitations are logged with reference numbers and RSVPs tracked; seating and
  order of arrival follow the precedence table.
- Accessibility needs, security screening, and dress code guidance are
  communicated clearly in advance.

## 6. Diplomatic engagements
- Note verbale and diplomatic letters follow established formats (see Section
  XXXVI templates) and must be authenticated via the Chancellery.
- Diplomatic gifts, if offered, are recorded and handled per protocol and
  ethics policies.

## 7. Records and compliance
- All formal correspondence and invitation lists are archived; sensitive guest
  data follows data protection rules.
- Deviations from protocol require written approval and must be noted in the
  event or correspondence file.
