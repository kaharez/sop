# X. PROCEDURAL APPENDICES

Appendix A — Workflow for Royal Decrees

## 1. Drafting request initiated by competent Office with authority clause.

## 2. Preparation by Royal Chancellery using approved templates and numbering.

## 3. Legal review by Office of Laws & Justice; revisions returned if needed.

## 4. Final text submitted to the Secretary of State; briefing to the Sovereign.

## 5. Sovereign approval; Great Seal affixed where required.

## 6. Registration in the Register of Instruments; Gazette publication with
   reference number and metadata.

Appendix B — Gazette Publication Standards
- Document formatting: clear headings, issuing authority, date, reference.
- Metadata: instrument type, subject, issuing Office, classification.
- Authentication: Chancellery validation, digital signature, and checksum.
- Archival: permanent storage with redundancy and public access where lawful.

Appendix C — Identity Documentation Workflow
- Application: submitted via ePortal or verified physical intake.
- Verification: identity proofing, eligibility checks, and fraud screening.
- NIN assignment: unique, non-reusable, logged with time and issuer.
- Issuance: secure credential (physical/digital) with tamper safeguards.
- Registry entry: audited, backed up, and available for authorised verification.

Appendix D — Heraldic Grant Workflow
- Petition: formal request with biography, symbolism intent, and references.
- Heraldic research: uniqueness check and compliance with heraldic law.
- Achievement design: collaborative drafting with Institute of Heraldry.
- Registration: entry into Registers of Arms/Flags/Badges with blazon text.
- Publication: Gazette notice and archival deposit of art assets.

Appendix E — Honours Nomination Process
- Submission: nominations accepted from Offices or accredited bodies.
- Vetting: eligibility, service evidence, and conflict-of-interest screening.
- Council review: recommendation recorded with citation draft.
- Sovereign approval: formalised via Royal Message or Decree.
- Investiture: scheduled by Ceremonies & Protocol; Gazette publication.

Appendix F — Protocol for State Ceremonies
- Precedence: apply current table; exceptions documented by Protocol Office.
- Seating: diagrams approved in advance; accessibility considered.
- Flags and symbols: displayed per national symbol protocol; condition checked.
- Dress and regalia: attire specified by event category; insignia verified.
- Procession: timed order of entry/exit rehearsed; contingencies documented.

Appendix G — Court Warrant Regulations
- Criteria: quality, reliability, ethical sourcing, alignment with state values.
- Application: submitted via Royal Court with references and product samples.
- Review/approval: assessed by Household and Institute of Heraldry; warrant
  issued by Sovereign.
- Heraldic badge: produced to standard; permitted placements specified.
- Renewal: annual review; revocation possible for breach or decline in quality.

Appendix H — Forms of Address
- Sovereign: “Your Serene Highness” or “Your Highness.”
- Court Officers: use title (e.g., “Master of the Household”).
- Government Officials: “Secretary,” “Undersecretary,” “Director” as relevant.
- Diplomats: rank-based forms (Ambassador, Envoy, Counsellor).
- Written correspondence: include titles, institutional address, and date.
