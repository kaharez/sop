# XXXI. EXPANDED SECTION: TABLE OF PRECEDENCE

## 1. Purpose
The Table of Precedence determines the order in which individuals appear,
are seated, are announced, and participate in ceremonial events.

## 3. Modifications for Specific Events

### A. Diplomatic Events
Foreign ambassadors may take precedence immediately after the Secretary of State.

### B. Honours Ceremonies
Recipients of the highest class of an Order precede recipients of lower classes.

### C. Court Events
Court Officers may take precedence within their own institution.

## 4. Ties in Rank

- **Resolved according to:**
- seniority in office,
- date of honour or appointment,
- specific instructions issued by the Office of Protocol.

## 5. Maintenance and publication
- The Office of State Ceremonies & Protocol maintains the master table and
  updates it upon new appointments, honours, or structural changes.
- Changes of general application are published in the Gazette; event-specific
  adjustments are recorded in the event file.

## 6. Special considerations
- Foreign dignitaries: precedence set in consultation with Foreign Affairs.
- Religious or cultural representatives: placed in consultation with event
  leads to respect inclusivity while preserving state precedence.
- Conflicts: disputes are resolved by the Office of Protocol and, if needed,
  escalated to the Secretary of State.
