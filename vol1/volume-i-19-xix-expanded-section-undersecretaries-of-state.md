# XIX. EXPANDED SECTION: UNDERSECRETARIES OF STATE

## 1. Role
Each Undersecretary of State leads one Section of the Secretariat and acts
as a senior executive responsible for policy, operations, and personnel.

## 2. Responsibilities (Expanded)

### A. Section Management
- Directs the Offices within the Section.
- Reviews reports and allocates priorities.
- Ensures coherence with national strategy.

### B. Policy Development
- Drafts policy proposals for review by the Secretary of State.
- Consults with Directors and specialists.

### C. Representation

- **Acts as the internal spokesperson of the Section in:**
- Coordination meetings
- Inter-Section projects
- Briefings before the Sovereign or Secretary of State

### D. Institutional Compliance

- **Ensures that Offices adhere to:**
- Legal standards
- Procedural guidelines
- Ethical requirements
- Archival obligations

## 3. Delivery and reporting
- Produces monthly Section reports covering outputs, risks, and resource needs
  for the Secretary of State.
- Maintains a Section workplan with clear owners and timelines; flags inter-
  Section dependencies early for coordination.
- Oversees budget stewardship for their Section where applicable, with
  transparent procurement aligned to standards.

## 4. Risk and quality management
- Ensures all instruments and programmes from the Section comply with legal,
  protocol, and data-protection standards.
- Conducts periodic reviews of Offices for performance, documentation quality,
  and adherence to Gazette and archival rules.

## 5. Succession and delegation
- Designates deputies within the Section to ensure continuity during absence
  or crisis; records delegation terms and duration.
- Leads Section participation in continuity of government exercises, ensuring
  that critical services have tested backup procedures.
