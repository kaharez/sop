# II. THE ROYAL COURT OF KAHARAGIA

The Royal Court is the ceremonial, symbolic, and household institution that
supports the Sovereign personally. It is separate from the governmental
Secretariat of State.

Overseen by the <mark>**Marshal of the Court**</mark>.

### A. Household of the Sovereign
- **Master of the Household:** oversees operations of the Court.
- **Keeper of the Privy Purse:** manages financial matters of the Court.
- **Private Secretary to the Sovereign:** handles personal correspondence.
- **Royal Archivist:** maintains historical records of the Court.

### B. Royal Protocol (works closely with Secretariat Section I)
- Manages audiences, ceremonies, receptions, and precedence.
- Advises on dress codes, etiquette, ceremonial orders.
- Coordinates protocol for diplomatic visits.

### C. Purveyors to the Royal Court
Companies or individuals granted a Royal Warrant to supply goods/services
of exceptional quality. The privilege is overseen jointly by:
- The Royal Chancellery (documentation and warrants)
- The Institute of Heraldry (design of warrant badges)

Purveryors are published annually in the Royal Gazette.

### D. Court Officers and Honour Guards (future)
Ceremonial officers, Masters of Ceremonies, heralds, and symbolic guards.
