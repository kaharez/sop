# XXIX. EXPANDED SECTION: OFFICE OF DIGITAL GOVERNMENT & CYBERSECURITY

## 1. Purpose
This Office ensures that the government remains secure, efficient, and accessible
through modern technology.

## 2. Responsibilities

### A. Digital Governance
- Develops secure electronic systems,
- supports online services through the ePortal,
- ensures accessibility and reliability.

### B. Identity Security

## Protects
- digital identity systems,
- data integrity,
- authentication protocols.

### C. Cybersecurity
Monitors threats, responds to incidents, and coordinates national cybersecurity
policy.

### D. Data Policy

## Establishes
- privacy protections,
- archiving standards,
- secure communication guidelines.

## 3. Architecture and standards
- Implements zero-trust principles for all government systems, enforcing
  role-based access control, MFA, encryption at rest and in transit.
- Maintains the digital identity layer and PKI for authenticated government
  transactions and signatures.
- Sets backup, disaster recovery, and business continuity requirements; tests
  regularly with incident simulations.

## 4. Operations and monitoring
- Runs a security operations capability to detect, triage, and respond to
  threats; coordinates with Defence Policy on national-level incidents.
- Maintains asset inventories, configuration baselines, and patch management
  schedules across government systems.

## 5. Data governance
- Issues data classification, retention, and disposal standards; ensures two-
  person controls on data destruction and access logs for audit.
- Works with Government Communications and the Royal Chancellery to ensure
  digital publications carry integrity metadata (hashes, signatures).

## 6. Service delivery
- Ensures the ePortal and citizen-facing services meet availability, latency,
  and accessibility targets; publishes status dashboards where appropriate.
- Provides technical guidance to other Offices to integrate securely with
  shared services and identity systems.
