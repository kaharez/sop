# LV. SUPPLEMENTARY INSTITUTIONAL GLOSSARY

This glossary defines key terms used throughout Volume I.

#### A. “Sovereign”
The head of state, representing continuity and unity.

#### B. “Secretariat of State”
The primary executive organ of the Principality overseeing governance,
coordination, and administration.

#### C. “Royal Chancellery”
The institution responsible for authentication, recording, sealing, and
publishing official instruments.

#### D. “Gazette”
The official publication of the State where all formal instruments, notices,
and announcements are recorded.

#### E. “Office”
A specialised administrative body within a Section of the Secretariat.

#### F. “Section”
A broad thematic division grouping related Offices under shared oversight.

#### G. “Purveyor”
A supplier or service provider formally authorised by Royal Warrant.

#### H. “Register”
An authoritative record maintained by the State for identity, honours, arms,
or other legally recognised data.

#### I. “Ceremonial”
Actions relating to official state functions, protocols, and traditions.

#### J. “Identity Architecture”
The system used to record, verify, and protect personal identity and
affiliation with the Principality.

#### K. “Continuity of Government (COG)”
Measures that preserve lawful authority, decision-making, and records during
disruptions or crises.

#### L. “Gazette Notice”
An authenticated publication in the Royal Gazette, including decrees,
appointments, ceremonial announcements, or archival notices.

#### M. “Great Seal”
The principal seal of the Sovereign, affixed by the Royal Chancellery to the
highest-level instruments requiring maximum authority.

#### N. “Register”
Any authoritative record (identity, honours, arms, instruments) maintained by
the State with controlled access, integrity checks, and audit trails.
