# XIII. EXPANDED SECTION: ROYAL CHANCELLERY

### 1. Purpose
The Royal Chancellery ensures the proper creation, authentication, and custody
of all instruments of state. It serves as the documentary heart of the
Principalityâ€™s governance.

### 2. Functions (Expanded)

#### A. Drafting and Preparation

### Documents are drafted in consultation with
- The Office of Laws & Justice
- Relevant Directors or Undersecretaries
- The Secretary of State

#### B. Authentication

### Every official instrument must be
- Reviewed for legality
- Formally registered
- Sealed appropriately
- Archived after publication

#### C. The Great Seal
The Chancellery maintains the Great Seal, which is affixed only to documents

### requiring the highest level of authority, such as
- Letters Patent
- Constitutional instruments
- Major appointments
- Honours of high rank

#### D. Legislative Process

### The Royal Chancellery ensures
- Receipt of proposed legal instruments
- Verification of their constitutional form
- Presentation to the Sovereign
- Recording in the official Register
- Gazette publication

### 3. Publication and record integrity
- Numbering: assigns unique reference numbers and ensures sequential integrity
  across Decrees, Letters Patent, Warrants, and Gazette notices.
- Metadata: records issuing authority, date, subject, classification, and
  related instruments; maintains repeal and amendment tables.
- Digital authenticity: applies approved digital signatures and checksums to
  all publications; maintains hash registers for audit.
- Repositories: keeps redundant archives (primary, secondary, cold storage)
  with periodic integrity checks; coordinates with National Archives.

### 4. Access and confidentiality
- Public instruments are published in the Gazette; sensitive instruments are
  stored with restricted access per data classification rules.
- Certified copies: issues certified extracts on request, with audit logs of
  access and reproduction.

### 5. Interfaces and workflows
- Works with Office of Laws & Justice for legal sufficiency and with Government
  Communications for publication formatting and timing.
- Coordinates with Institute of Heraldry for seal usage, heraldic accuracy,
  and protection of state symbols.
- Supports the Office of State Ceremonies & Protocol with ceremonial scripts
  and authenticated documents for events.
