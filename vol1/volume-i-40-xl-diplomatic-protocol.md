# XL. DIPLOMATIC PROTOCOL

## 1. Diplomatic Accreditation

## The Sovereign accredits ambassadors upon recommendation of
- **Office of Foreign Affairs**
- Secretary of State

## 2. Letters of Credence

## Must include
- Sovereign’s signature
- Great Seal
- authentication by Royal Chancellery

## 3. Diplomatic Rank

## Recognised ranks include
- Ambassador
- Envoy
- Counsellor
- Attaché

## 4. Diplomatic Correspondence

## All official communications must
- follow formal diplomatic style,
- avoid political commentary unless authorised,
- be archived appropriately.

## 5. Presentation of credentials
- Scheduling and format coordinated by State Ceremonies & Protocol with the
  Royal Court; seating and procession follow precedence rules.
- Letters of credence/recall are authenticated by the Royal Chancellery and
  recorded in the Gazette.

## 6. Visits and engagements
- Itineraries are agreed with Foreign Affairs; security assessments conducted
  with Defence Policy.
- Gift policy follows protocol and ethics rules; items recorded and, where
  appropriate, entered into Royal Collections.

## 7. Communication and records
- Diplomatic notes and correspondence use approved templates (see Section
  XXXVI) and are registered for archival completeness.
- Summaries of significant engagements may be published in the Gazette to
  maintain transparency and public awareness.
