# LXIV. DIPLOMATIC IMAGE & REPUTATION MANAGEMENT

## 1. Purpose
To safeguard the Principality’s credibility, dignity, and international presence.

## 2. Reputation Principles

### A. Consistency
All communications must uphold a coherent image of professionalism and dignity.

### B. Non‑Political Posture
Kaharagia must avoid involvement in partisan or territorial disputes.

### C. Reliability

## Foreign entities must be able to rely on
- predictable protocol,
- professional correspondence,
- accurate record keeping.

## 3. Reputation Protection Measures

### A. Correcting Misrepresentation
False information must be addressed swiftly and formally when necessary.

### B. Controlled Use of Public Statements
Only authorised offices may issue statements on sensitive issues.

### C. Documentation Integrity
Meticulous publication practices (especially in the Gazette) enhance credibility.

## 4. Monitoring and response
- Monitor media and digital channels for misinformation affecting legitimacy or
  identity; coordinate factual corrections through Government Communications.
- Maintain a rapid response protocol for reputational risks, with pre-cleared
  spokespersons and approved messaging.

## 5. Roles and approvals
- Foreign Affairs leads on international image; Government Communications
  manages messaging; Laws & Justice reviews legal exposure; Protocol ensures
  dignity and consistency.
- Sensitive statements require clearance from the Secretary of State.

## 6. Consistency across touchpoints
- Ensure ceremonial, diplomatic, and digital representations use authorised
  symbols, titles, and formats; deviations are corrected promptly.
- Gazette publications remain the authoritative reference to counter
  conflicting narratives.
