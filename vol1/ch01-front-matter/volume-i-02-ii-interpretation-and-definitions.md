# II. INTERPRETATION AND DEFINITIONS

- **For the purposes of this Manual:**

“Sovereign” refers to the head of state of the Principality of Kaharagia.

“Royal Court” refers to the ceremonial, symbolic, and administrative household
serving the Sovereign personally, distinct from the governmental apparatus.

“Secretariat of State” is the principal executive institution responsible for
the governance and administration of national affairs.

“Section” refers to a major division within the Secretariat of State,
each headed by an Undersecretary of State.

“Office” refers to a functional department within a Section.

“Director” means the head of an Office.

“Deputy Secretary of State” refers to the head of a special institution
attached to the Secretariat of State.

“Royal Chancellery” refers to the body responsible for Royal Decrees, Letters
Patent, official seals, and documentary authentication.

“Chancellery of Honours” refers to the institution managing Orders, Decorations,
Medals, and the Register of Honours.

“Institute of Heraldry” refers to the institution responsible for heraldic
grants, national symbolism, and the Register of Arms, Flags, and Badges.

“Royal Gazette” refers to the official publication through which the government
promulgates decrees, notices, and official acts.

“National Identity Number (NIN)” refers to the unique identifying number
assigned to each citizen.

“Great Seal” refers to the principal seal of the Sovereign, affixed by the
Royal Chancellery to instruments requiring the highest authority.

“Continuity of Government (COG)” refers to measures that preserve lawful
authority and records during crises or disruptions.

“Digital Identity” refers to the authenticated credentials issued or recognised
by the Principality for secure access to government systems.

“Gazette Notice” refers to any authenticated publication in the Royal Gazette,
including decrees, appointments, ceremonial announcements, and archival notices.

- **Interpretive guidance:**
- Capitalised terms have the meanings above unless context demands otherwise.
- Where terminology overlaps across Sections, the stricter or more specific
  definition applies.
- In case of ambiguity, the Office of Laws & Justice provides authoritative
  interpretation, recorded by the Royal Chancellery.
