# XXIII. EXPANDED SECTION: OFFICE OF LAWS & JUSTICE

1. Purpose
The Office of Laws & Justice is responsible for safeguarding legal integrity
across all branches of governance. It ensures that all official actions comply
with constitutional principles, established procedures, and the Sovereignâ€™s
legal authority.

2. Core Responsibilities
A. Legal Counsel
Advises the Sovereign, Secretary of State, Undersecretaries, and Directors on:
- interpretation of decrees,
- legality of administrative measures,
- drafting of new legal instruments.

B. Legislative Drafting
Supports the Royal Chancellery by preparing:
- Royal Decrees,
- Charters,
- Letters Patent,
- amendments to institutional frameworks.

C. Codification
Maintains up-to-date compilations of:
- Royal Decrees in force,
- administrative policies,
- procedural guidelines,
- archival legal records.

D. Compliance Oversight
Ensures that:
- all Offices follow approved procedures,
- documentation meets required legal standards,
- Gazette publications reflect authentic and lawful decisions.

3. Legal Review Procedure
The Office must:
1. Receive draft from an Office or Section.
2. Conduct legal review for clarity, authority, and compliance.
3. Return recommendations for revision, if required.
4. Approve the draft for Chancellery submission.
5. Track legal instruments after publication.

4. Guardian of Administrative Ethics
The Office promotes:
- impartiality,
- procedural fairness,
- proportionality,
- non-discrimination,
- responsible decision-making.

5. Litigation and dispute resolution
- Manages legal correspondence and dispute responses; coordinates with the
  Royal Chancellery on official positions and records.
- Advises on dispute resolution mechanisms between Offices or external parties,
  documenting settlements or decisions for archival.

6. Templates and standards
- Maintains model clauses, authority statements, and citation standards for all
  legal instruments.
- Provides training to Directors and drafters on legal style, clarity, and
  compliance with drafting standards (Section XLVI).

7. Accountability and audit
- Reviews compliance with Gazette publication requirements and numbering; flags
  irregularities for corrective action.
- Maintains a log of advisory opinions and legal clearances to ensure traceable
  decision-making.
