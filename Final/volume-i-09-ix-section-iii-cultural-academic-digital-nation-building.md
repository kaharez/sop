# IX. SECTION III — CULTURAL, ACADEMIC & DIGITAL NATION-BUILDING

Offices:
1. Office of Culture, Language & Heritage
2. Office of Education, Science & Innovation
3. Office of Digital Government & Cybersecurity

Expanded Functions:
- Governance of national digital identity systems.
- Oversight of cultural preservation projects.
- Development of educational partnerships.

Operational focus and outputs:
- Office of Culture, Language & Heritage
  - Curates national symbols, language standards, and cultural programming.
  - Manages heritage archives and coordinates with the Royal Chancellery for
    preservation of ceremonial records and artefacts.
  - Runs public culture initiatives (festivals, exhibitions, publications).

- Office of Education, Science & Innovation
  - Develops curriculum guidance highlighting governance, history, and civic
    identity; partners with institutions for research and scholarships.
  - Sponsors innovation grants and recognises scientific contributions through
    honours and Gazette features.
  - Oversees digital learning platforms and accessibility standards.

- Office of Digital Government & Cybersecurity
  - Owns the ePortal, digital identity layer, and zero-trust security
    architecture for government systems.
  - Implements incident response, backup, and disaster recovery for critical
    services; coordinates with Section II for national security incidents.
  - Maintains data classification policies, access controls, and encryption
    baselines across all Offices.

Interfaces:
- Works with Institute of Heraldry on cultural and symbolic materials.
- Aligns education and innovation programmes with external relations to
  showcase national achievements.
- Shares data standards and threat intelligence with Section I (identity) and
  Section II (security) to ensure unified safeguards.
