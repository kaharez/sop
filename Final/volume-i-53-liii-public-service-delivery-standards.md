# LIII. PUBLIC SERVICE DELIVERY STANDARDS

1. Purpose
To ensure all interactions between individuals and the State are handled with
professionalism, efficiency, and courtesy.

2. Service Charter Principles
A. Accessibility
Public services must be easy to access via:
- digital portals,
- written correspondence,
- ceremonial or official channels (as appropriate).

B. Timeliness
Government Offices must publish expected response times.

C. Transparency
Clear information regarding processes, requirements, and timelines.

D. Courtesy
Personnel must act with respect, clarity, and professionalism.

3. Service Categories
A. Identity and Citizenship Services
Processing applications, status updates, document issuance.

B. Honours and Recognition Services
Nominations, registry updates, ceremonial arrangements.

C. Cultural and Heritage Services
Access to archives, participation in programmes.

D. Administrative Requests
Correspondence, clarification, and publication requests.

4. Complaint Resolution
Complaints must be:
- acknowledged promptly,
- assigned a tracking reference,
- investigated fairly,
- resolved in writing.

5. Metrics and monitoring
- Each service category defines service-level targets; performance is reported
  monthly to Undersecretaries.
- User feedback is captured and reviewed; improvement actions are tracked.

6. Accessibility and inclusion
- Services must be available via digital and, where needed, alternative
  channels; accommodations are provided for language, disability, and access
  constraints.
- Content must be clear, plain-language, and consistent with Gazette versions.

7. Security and privacy
- Identity-sensitive services follow multi-factor authentication and data
  minimisation; logs are maintained for accountability.
- Public interactions avoid collecting unnecessary personal data; retention
  limits are applied.
